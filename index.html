Research Interests

I am interested in how Human-Centered Affordances play a role in Algorithmic Systems and their Applications. As such, my research spans both the Theoretical and Applied domains. If you'd like to learn more about my research, feel free to reach out via email or Twitter!

My Theoretical CS (TCS) work (advised by Prof. Chandra Chekuri) centers around

  • Approximation Algorithms and Optimization
  • Fairness in Algorithm Design, Fair Clustering

My Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) work (advised by Prof. Eshwar Chandrasekharan, the PI of SCUBA LAB) focuses on

  • Social Computing, Data Science, and Human-Centered AI
  • Content Moderation on Text- and Audio- Based Platforms

Hi hello!

I am currently a second year PhD student at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign.

My area of interest is theoretical computer science (fair algorithm design, approximation algorithms) and human-computer interaction (content moderation on text- and audio-based platforms). I am currently working with Chandra Chekuri and Eshwar Chandrasekharan. As an undergraduate, I worked with Professor R. Ravi on projects relating to algorithm design for recommendation system diversification and student-question ranking problems. During the summer of 2018, I worked with Professor Samir Khuller on fair clustering algorithms.

I was formerly an undergraduate studying Computer Science and Discrete Mathematics and Logic at Carnegie Mellon University.

I grew up in Princeton Junction, New Jersey, which is around ten minutes outside of Princeton University. During high school, I had the opportunity to participate in the Program for Algorithmic and Combinatorial Thinking, which inspired my love for theory and discrete math.
