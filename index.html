Hi hello!

I am currently a senior studying Computer Science and Discrete Mathematics and Logic at Carnegie Mellon University.

Starting Fall 2019 I will be a PhD student at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign.

My area of interest is theoretical computer science, in particular, algorithms and combinatorial optimization used to solve real world problems. As an undergraduate, I worked with Professor R. Ravi on projects relating to algorithm design for recommendation system diversification and student-question ranking problems. During the summer of 2018, I worked with Professor Samir Khuller on fair clustering algorithms.

I grew up in Princeton Junction, New Jersey, which is around ten minutes outside of Princeton University. During high school, I had the opportunity to participate in the Program for Algorithmic and Combinatorial Thinking, which inspired my love for theory and discrete math.

Passions

I am absurdly passionate about diversity and representation in Computer Science. I have been an active member of W@SCS and SCS4ALL while at CMU. Some cool things I've planned for W@SCS include

  • Secrets From the Loud Girls Panel

    A panel where women student leaders open up about their experiences with imposterism, low-self confidence, and how they have (or have not) overcome those feelings.

  • Teaching Assistant Panel

    A panel for anyone interested in being a TA. Panel members happen to all be women, since its original purpose was to encourage more women to try for TA positions. Many courses have seen an increase in women who express interest in TAing after the conception of this panel (correlation != causation, but that was really good news to hear!)

I am equally passionate about CS education and pedagogy, and have been lucky to have Anil Ada as my academic advisor and mentor. I pester him a lot with pedagogical debates and ideas. I may start a blog with more of my thoughts about this topic (no promises though since I am (1) not really good at following through on things I propose and (2) still a student and somewhat not qualified). Ada and Umut Acar won an award for Diderot, an "online textbook" learning system, read more about it here!

I am also very passionate about bringing awareness to mental health. Mental health is important, and I have my personal struggles with it. Again, I may or may not go into more detail about this on a hypothetical blog. We'll see.
