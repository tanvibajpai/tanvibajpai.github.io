``Theory and HCI? That's an interesting combo...''

I began my PhD journey with a focus on TCS research, driven by my passion for mathematics and algorithm design. This passion, coupled with my drive to tackle relevant real-world challenges, led me to explore how to better incorporate diversity and fairness in algorithms. As I delved deeper into this topic, I realized that to fully understand how algorithms function in real-world contexts—and how they can be used to benefit people—I needed to engage in more human-facing, interdisciplinary research. This insight led me to pursue work in HCI, where I concentrated on online community governance and moderation.


Throughout the course of my PhD, I developed and refined skills and insights from each field separately and critically examined how my expertise in each area could be combined. As a result, I have gained a novel perspective on how research tools from TCS and HCI can be integrated to effectively design and analyze socio-technical algorithmic systems.


In addition to my academic pursuits, I spent 15 months as a Data Science Research Intern at AliveCor, where I honed my skills in Machine Learning, AI, and Data Analysis. This experience not only enhanced my research and analytical toolkit but also introduced me to a new application domain. This opportunity allowed me to learn about a new field and identify and address open problems within it.


Overall, I am a researcher who integrates skills from CS Theory, HCI, and ML/AI. This versatility equips me to tackle a wide range of problem spaces, making me a strong interdisciplinary researcher who is excited to work on new challenges and innovative problems.
